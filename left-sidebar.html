Assignment 1: POSTER

Get the pdf version

On November 14th, a number of successful individuals in the ICT industry hosted an online the first Industry Talk event - Overview of ICT Jobs in Clarity Techworks, SDN BHD. The five presenters included the chief technology officer, a QA test analyst, a junior Android developer, a junior UI/UX designer, and a junior web developer who had previously attended UTM. The first part of the discussion discusses how technology is always evolving and how breakthroughs in software, cybersecurity, and artificial intelligence are affecting the globe.

A junior Android wizard spills the beans on their daily gig, featuring tools like Android Studio, Google Play Console, and the magical Bugfender and Fresh. They dish out the deets on logic skills, good communication vibes, efficient code crafting, and the ever-essential art of continuous learning

Advice:

Love What You Do: Especially directed at software engineering students, the advice is to question why they chose IT. Loving what you do is the anchor that keeps you committed and engaged, even when faced with challenges. Be a Self-Learner: Reinforcing the earlier point, the speaker suggests that relying on formal education alone isn't sufficient. Being a self-learner, someone who actively seeks out knowledge and skills, is positioned for long-term success.

Important Skills - Passion for the Work: The speaker underscores the importance of genuinely loving what you do. In the tech world, this translates to a passion for coding, problem-solving, and creating innovative solutions. It's not just about fulfilling job requirements but about being genuinely interested and excited about the work. Self-Learning: A key takeaway is the need to be a self-learner. In the ever-evolving tech landscape, waiting for someone to teach you is not an option. Those who proactively seek knowledge, learn independently, and stay updated are the ones who stand out. Continuous learning is the heartbeat of a successful career.

So in conclusion ...

In essence, the advice and emphasis on these skills paint a picture of a dynamic and challenging work environment where personal commitment, continuous learning, and a collaborative spirit are the keys to thriving
